PromptSynth

Evolving Supercollider synths using Large Language Models

The Narrative

Since the launch of LLMs, I've been testing them by trying to generate Supercollider (SC) Code. Fast forward to June 2024 when Claude 3.5 became the first to successfully generate working code. Time to play!

Exploration Journey

  • Began testing the depth of understanding in generating SC code
  • Surprisingly successful at different prompt strategies for different outcomes:
    • Synth construction
      • Describing a synth design with parameters

        • Synth evolution example

      • Modeling hardware synths (e.g., Juno-8, MoogOne, Buchla, ArpOdyssey)
      • Descriptive approach (texture, object, environment) such as:
        • Metal, wood, Melbourne Pedestrian Crossing
        • Raindrop, Whip Bird, Forest, Cricket, Cicada, Interactive Wind
    • Music construction
      • Steve Reich, Brian Eno, Stockhausen, Xenakis
  • "Vibe coding" (inspired by Andrej Karpathy): continued to use this approach in all synth designs, from building scaffolding code to abstract descriptions
  • 2025: The need to automate with Claude's API. $ign me up!
  • Hacked a basic workflow to evolve synths:
    • Start with a prototype synth
    • Give a prompt (used for every iteration)
    • Hit go: (calls to SC to load, interpret and play new Synth Definition)
    • Each iteration is saved and then loaded into SC, so every iteration is archived

Outcomes So Far

  • Very successful in creating a family of sounds that evolve over each iteration
  • Errors propagate but sometimes disappear after several iterations
  • Feels like I am growing synths
  • Have not improved or developed the idea further as I am too busy 'prompting' new ideas and listening. Is this a good thing?
